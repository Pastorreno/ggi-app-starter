# GGI Design OS

## Purpose
GGI Design OS is the reusable design and content layer for GGI Hub applications, client portals, landing pages, dashboards, AI tools, event pages, and proof-of-concept products.

It is inspired by the design-skill concept behind tools like huashu-design, but it is adapted for GGI Hub's actual business model: practical, professional, conversion-focused, and ready for churches, nonprofits, schools, community organizations, and small businesses.

The goal is not just to make things look good. The goal is to turn ideas into clear, credible, polished digital products that can be sold, demonstrated, deployed, and reused.

---

## Core Identity

GGI Hub should feel like:

- Modern
- Clean
- Strategic
- Human-centered
- Trustworthy
- AI-powered without feeling gimmicky
- Kingdom-aligned when appropriate, but not overly churchy by default
- Built for practical leaders who need systems, not hype

Avoid:

- Cheap AI graphics
- Overcrowded layouts
- Generic SaaS wording
- Cartoonish church visuals
- Overuse of buzzwords
- Designs that look impressive but do not explain the offer clearly

---

## Default Design Principles

### 1. Clarity First
Every page must answer:

- What is this?
- Who is it for?
- What problem does it solve?
- What should the user do next?

### 2. Trust Before Flash
GGI Hub is selling systems, leadership, automation, and clarity. The design should build confidence before it tries to impress.

### 3. Offer-First Design
Do not design around decoration. Design around the offer, the audience, and the next action.

### 4. Human + AI Balance
GGI Hub should make it clear that AI reduces administrative load, reveals patterns, and speeds up work, but humans remain central to leadership, care, judgment, and decision-making.

### 5. Reusable System Thinking
Every layout should be easy to reuse for multiple verticals:

- Churches
- Schools
- Youth programs
- Nonprofits
- Civic organizations
- Small businesses
- Real estate lead systems
- Event campaigns

## GGI Design Modes

Use one of these modes before generating a page, dashboard, proposal, or product mockup.

### 1. Kingdom Tech
Best for: GGI Hub, church systems, leadership pipeline, AI consulting, church portals.

Look and feel:

- Premium SaaS
- Deep charcoal, white, gold, warm neutral accents
- Clean cards
- Strong headings
- Confident but not flashy

Content tone:

- Strategic
- Clear
- Faith-aware but not heavy-handed
- Practical and systems-oriented

Use when the offer needs to feel like a serious technology solution for ministry or leadership.

---

### 2. Community Impact
Best for: youth programs, nonprofit dashboards, school pilots, civic projects, community development.

Look and feel:

- Warm
- Approachable
- People-centered
- Simple visuals
- Clear impact metrics

Content tone:

- Hopeful
- Practical
- Community-centered
- Outcome-focused

Use when the audience needs to feel safety, trust, and local impact.

---

### 3. Executive Proposal
Best for: school boards, pastors, funders, investors, civic leaders, corporate-style presentations.

Look and feel:

- Clean consulting deck style
- White space
- Strong section hierarchy
- Metrics and framework diagrams
- Minimal decoration

Content tone:

- Precise
- Strategic
- Confident
- Evidence-oriented

Use when the asset needs to be taken seriously by decision-makers.

---

### 4. Street-to-Structure
Best for: Go Get It Motivation, mentoring, youth development, reentry, discipline-based leadership programs.

Look and feel:

- Bold typography
- Urban but polished
- Strong contrast
- Direct messaging
- Movement and momentum

Content tone:

- Direct
- Motivational
- Accountable
- Gritty but professional

Use when the message needs to connect with people who need discipline, purpose, and structure.

---

### 5. Prophetic Worship
Best for: SAW, worship events, music visuals, creative collectives, worship nights, promo pages.

Look and feel:

- Cinematic
- Dark backgrounds
- Light beams / atmosphere
- Elegant typography
- Minimal but emotionally strong

Content tone:

- Reverent
- Creative
- Invitational
- Spiritual without being corny

Use when the design should feel like an experience, not just an announcement.

Use this prompt in ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Kiro, or another coding/design assistant.

```text
You are GGI Design OS, a premium AI design strategist for GGI Hub.

Your job is to turn rough ideas into polished, high-converting digital assets.

Brand identity:
- Modern
- Professional
- Practical
- Human-centered
- Kingdom-aligned when appropriate, but not overly churchy by default
- Built for leaders, churches, schools, nonprofits, small businesses, and community organizations

1. Clarify the purpose of the asset.
2. Identify the target audience.
3. Select the best design mode:
   - Kingdom Tech
   - Community Impact
   - Executive Proposal
   - Street-to-Structure
   - Prophetic Worship
4. Structure the message around the user's problem, the offered solution, proof, and next action.
5. Generate polished copy and a clean layout.
6. If code is requested, generate responsive HTML/CSS or Next.js + Tailwind components.
7. Review the output using the GGI quality rubric.

Always prioritize:
- clarity
- credibility
- conversion
- emotional connection
- brand alignment
- reusable structure

Avoid:
- generic hype
- unclear CTAs
- clutter
- vague AI language
- designs that look impressive but fail to explain the offer

Final output should include:
- Design mode selected
- Page or asset structure
- Copy
- Visual direction
- CTA strategy
- Quality review
- Suggested next improvement
```

## GGI Quality Rubric

Score every asset from 1-10.

```text
Clarity:
Does the audience understand what this is and why it matters?

Credibility:
Does this feel trustworthy, professional, and serious?

Visual Strength:
Does the layout feel polished, modern, and intentional?

Emotional Pull:
Does the message connect with the audience's real need?

Conversion Power:
Is the next action obvious and compelling?

Brand Fit:
Does it feel like GGI Hub and not a generic template?

Human + AI Balance:
Does it show AI as a helpful tool without replacing human leadership?

Reusable Value:
Can this structure be adapted for future clients or offers?

Final Grade:
[Average score]

Fix Before Publishing:
[List the top 3 improvements]
```

## Final Standard

A GGI Design OS asset is ready when it can clearly answer:

1. What are we offering?
2. Who is it for?
3. Why does it matter now?
4. How does it work?
5. What does the user do next?
6. Can this be reused for another client or vertical?

If the asset cannot answer those six questions, it is not ready to publish.
